What is Kunitachi like?

Kunitachi City is located in the central part of Tokyo, Japan. It borders Fuchu City to the east, Tachikawa City to the west, Kokubunji City to the north, and Hino City across the Tama River to the south. It is the second smallest city in Tokyo and the fourth smallest city in Japan.

Despite being a small city, Kunitachi has many attractions.

As the first designated cultural and educational district in Tokyo, Kunitachi is home to numerous educational institutions such as universities and high schools. During the daytime, the city is vibrant with many students, but there are regulations on the construction of facilities that may impact education, giving the entire city a calm atmosphere. The main street of the city, known as Daigaku-dori, and its surrounding area are filled with trendy shops and restaurants, making it an enjoyable place to stroll around. When visiting Kunitachi, be sure to explore the unique shops scattered throughout the city and enjoy shopping.

Daigaku-dori, which extends straight south from JR Kunitachi Station, is the city's main street and is approximately 44 meters wide. Currently, cherry trees and gingko trees are planted alternately along the green belts on both sides of the road. In spring, the cherry blossoms create a curtain of pink petals, while in autumn, the golden leaves of the gingko trees radiate a brilliant glow. This scenery has been selected as one of the "New Tokyo Top 100 Views" and has become a symbol of Kunitachi.

If you want to experience a different side of Kunitachi, take a walk southward along Daigaku-dori and enjoy exploring the green and rural areas in the southern part of the city. It is a place where you can witness another charm of Kunitachi that is completely different from the main street.

Such a dual charm harmonizes within the compact space of 8.15 square kilometers. I encourage all of you to visit this city, filled with greenery and distinctive characteristics, at least once.
